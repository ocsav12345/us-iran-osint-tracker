In the immediate aftermath of last night's Iranian retaliation strike against Israeli territory, the headline number you have probably seen is "99% of the incoming munitions were intercepted." It has appeared in IDF briefings, in major Western wire reports, and in social media commentary across the political spectrum. This post is about what that number actually means, what it does not mean, and how to read it responsibly.

## What was launched

Per public Iranian and Israeli statements and corroborated open-source reporting, the package consisted of roughly 300 munitions of mixed types: a large number of Shahed-class loitering munitions (slow, low, observable), a smaller number of cruise missiles (medium speed, low to medium altitude), and a smaller number of ballistic missiles (very fast, high altitude, terminal phase short). The exact mix is still being established; numbers are likely to be revised over the coming days.

## What "99% intercepted" can mean

The phrasing is doing several different jobs at once:

### Interpretation 1: "99% did not impact populated areas of Israel"

This is plausibly true and is the strongest reading of the IDF statement. Most of the slow-moving Shaheds were intercepted en route by Israeli, US, UK, French, and Jordanian air defences before they entered Israeli airspace. Many of the ballistic missiles that did enter Israeli airspace were intercepted by Arrow before impact. The successful saturation of low-altitude defences against Shaheds and the high-altitude Arrow intercepts were both real, well-documented, and publicly attested by multiple military sources.

### Interpretation 2: "99% of all launched munitions failed to function as intended"

This is a different claim. It rolls into the 99% figure not just intercepts but also munitions that failed in flight (a non-trivial proportion of Shahed launches historically fail or crash short of target) and munitions intercepted outside Israeli airspace by allied air defences. By this reading, "intercept" is doing more work than it normally would in a defensive-systems context.

### Interpretation 3: "1% reached Israeli airspace and impacted"

The complementary statement: a small number of ballistic missiles reached Israeli territory and impacted, primarily at Nevatim airbase. This is consistent with both Israeli and Iranian framing — Iran framed the strike as "limited and precise," Israel framed the intercepts as "successful and comprehensive."

The three interpretations are not mutually exclusive and the IDF statement plausibly contains all three at once. Disentangling them matters because the operational lesson differs depending on which one you take to be the dominant reading.

## How to read this responsibly

A few discipline points for the next 72 hours:

1. **Anchor on impact locations, not on intercept percentages.** What got hit, what did not, where the craters are, what the damage signature looks like — this is the part open-source analysis can actually do. Percentages are a derived product; impacts are primary evidence.

2. **Note who was doing the intercepting.** The Iranian retaliation package was met by a multinational defensive coalition: Israeli air defence (Arrow, David's Sling, Iron Dome), US Navy ships in the Eastern Mediterranean, US and UK air assets, French air assets in the region, and Jordanian air defence over Jordanian airspace. The 99% figure is a multinational result, not a single-actor figure.

3. **Be careful about extrapolating to future engagements.** The shape of last night's engagement — long advance warning, slow Shaheds first, predictable trajectories — is one shape. A different engagement profile (no warning, ballistic-heavy, saturation) would produce a different result. The 99% does not generalise.

4. **Do not amplify intercept-rate claims as if they were the headline.** The headline is what happened, not what didn't. The intercept rate is a context number.

## What we expect to see in the next 24-72 hours

- **Damage assessment imagery from Nevatim.** Sentinel-2 and (probably) Planet Labs will give us the first usable looks. See the forthcoming [Nevatim damage assessment](../2026-05-11-nevatim-airbase-damage-assessment-preliminary/) for the work in progress.
- **Munition debris photographs.** Both Israeli authorities and ground-level civilians have been publishing fragments. Identification work is ongoing.
- **Casualty figures.** Currently reported as minimal direct casualties in Israel; we will see how that holds.
- **Iranian framing of the operation.** State media is already characterising the strike as completed and proportionate; this is consistent with the historical pattern set by 2020 al-Asad.
